Buoyancy

The buoyant force is the upward force exerted on an object by a fluid in which it is immersed. The magnitude of the buoyant force is equal to the weight of the displaced fluid. When an object is submerged in a fluid, it displaces a volume of that fluid equal to its own volume. The weight of this displaced fluid creates an upward force on the object that counteracts its gravitational pull downward.
